Welcome

This journal explores the intersection of legal theory and environmental protection in India. The primary focus is on how the sociological school of jurisprudence has shaped Indian environmental law through landmark Supreme Court and High Court decisions.

Featured Article: Sociological School & Indian Environmental Jurisprudence – Case Laws — An in-depth study of how courts have applied sociological principles to advance environmental rights in India, covering landmark cases from the 1980s to the present day.

Topics Covered

  • The Sociological School of Jurisprudence – origins, key thinkers, and core ideas
  • Evolution of environmental jurisprudence in India
  • Landmark case laws connecting sociological reasoning with environmental protection
  • The Public Trust Doctrine, Polluter Pays Principle, and Precautionary Principle in Indian courts
  • Right to a clean environment as part of Article 21 of the Indian Constitution
